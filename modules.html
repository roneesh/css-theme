Sections

You saw these in elements, so there's no need to go into detail. The section is a stylized block level container that we use predominantly in our designs.

I am a section, I can hold any html/css content.

<div class="section">
   ...content goes here
</div>

Header and Footer

Most sites have a Header and Footer of some kind, especialy ones that lean more towards web application than website. This themes Header is sticky at the top of the page, and then scrolls down its container. Other parts of the page might use a header, but in those cases they would just use the header tag with no class, and apply any styled child element they desire.

<header class="page-header">
   ... H tag or images go here.
</header>

<footer class="page-footer">
   ... small tag or images go here.
</footer>

Sliders

It's a fact of life, people love when elements "woosh" around the page. So a theme that can have elements slide in and out of view would be great. This theme uses a very lightweight implementation, it utilizes a few technologies:

  1. CSS Transforms and Animations
  2. The IntersectionObserver API

I'll slide in from the left when I enter the viewport.

<div class="section js-slider slide-left" data-slide-class="slide-in">
   <p>I slide in from the left</p>
</div>

I'll slide in from the right when I enter the viewport.

<div class="section js-slider slide-right" data-slide-class="slide-in">
   <p>I slide in from the right</p>
</div>

Alerts

Alerts are increasingly a part of web pages now that there are frequent AJAX calls that can fail or produce errors. A robust, global alert notification system is great for letting users know results of their actions, and what potential next steps that might take.

Let's try it out, send a sample alert here:

The global alerts markup is quite simple:

<div id="alerts-container">
   <div class="alert alert--primary">
      I am alert text
   </div>
</div>

Likewise, you could render an alert anywhere just by applying a class:

I am in inline alert!
<div id="alert alert--primary">
   I am an inline alert!
</div>
